Understanding Color Blindness

  • Protanopia/Protanomaly: Reduced sensitivity to red light ("red-weakness"). Reds may appear dark or muted.
  • Deuteranopia/Deuteranomaly: Reduced sensitivity to green light ("green-weakness"). This is the most common form of color blindness.
  • Tritanopia/Tritanomaly: Reduced sensitivity to blue light ("blue-weakness"). This is a rarer condition.
  • Achromatopsia: Complete color blindness, where everything is seen in shades of gray.
