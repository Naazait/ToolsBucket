How to Use the Scientific Calculator

Our calculator is designed to be intuitive, but here’s a quick guide to its features:

  1. Input & Display: The top display shows your current expression, while the larger bottom display shows the result. Simply click the buttons to build your equation.
  2. Basic Operations: Use the standard number buttons and operators (+, -, ×, ÷) for arithmetic.
  3. Scientific Functions:
    • Use sin, cos, and tan for trigonometric calculations. Remember to close the parenthesis, e.g., sin(90).
    • Use the √ button for square roots, e.g., √(16).
    • Use the xʸ button for exponents, e.g., 2**3 for 2 raised to the power of 3.
  4. Constants: Instantly insert the values for Pi (π) and Euler's number (e) with a single click.
  5. Control Buttons:
    • C: Clears the entire expression and result.
    • ⌫: Deletes the last character entered (backspace).
    • =: Evaluates the current expression and shows the final answer.
