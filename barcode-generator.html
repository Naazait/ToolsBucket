Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

We've compiled answers to some common questions about barcode generation and our tool:

What are barcodes used for?

Barcodes are primarily used for quick and accurate identification and tracking of items. They are essential in retail for point-of-sale systems, in logistics for inventory and shipment tracking, in healthcare for patient and medication identification, and in libraries for book management, among many other applications. They help automate data entry and reduce errors.

Which barcode format should I use?

The best barcode format depends on your specific application:

  • CODE128: Highly versatile, encodes alphanumeric data, and is widely used for shipping containers, asset tags, and general inventory.
  • EAN-13: Standard for retail products sold internationally (outside of North America). It encodes 13 digits and includes a check digit.
  • UPC-A: Primarily used for retail products in the United States and Canada. It encodes 12 digits, also with a check digit.
  • ITF-14: Often used for shipping units containing retail items, like cartons or pallets. It encodes numeric data and typically has 14 digits.
  • CODE39: A basic alphanumeric barcode, useful for internal applications and less dense data.

If you're unsure, CODE128 is a robust choice for most general-purpose applications.

Can I generate multiple barcodes at once?

Currently, our tool is designed to generate one barcode at a time. For generating multiple unique barcodes, you would input each data string individually and download them.

Do the barcodes generated here expire?

No, the barcodes created by this tool are static images that encode the data you provide. They do not have an expiration date. Once generated, they are permanent representations of your input data.

Is my data safe when using this tool?

Absolutely. Your privacy is a top priority. Our barcode generator performs all operations client-side, meaning the data you enter into the text field is processed directly in your web browser and is never uploaded to our servers. This ensures your information remains secure and private.

What is the recommended resolution for printing barcodes?

For most applications, downloading your barcode as an SVG (Scalable Vector Graphic) is recommended, as it maintains perfect quality at any size. If you need a raster image (PNG/JPG), aim for a higher resolution like 300 DPI or more, which can be achieved by using a higher scale factor during image conversion, as handled by our download function.
