Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What exactly are meta tags?
Meta tags are snippets of text that describe a page's content, but they don't appear on the page itself; they only exist in the page's source code. They are "meta" because they provide metadata (data about data) about your webpage. Key meta tags include the title tag, meta description, and Open Graph tags, which tell search engines and social media platforms what your page is about.
Why are meta tags important for SEO?
Meta tags are crucial for SEO because they communicate important information about your webpage to search engines. The title tag is a significant ranking factor, and both the title and meta description influence click-through rates (CTR) from search results. Well-optimized meta tags help search engines understand your content better and present it attractively to users, driving more relevant traffic to your site.
What's the difference between a title tag and a meta description?
The **title tag** (``) defines the title of your document and is displayed in the browser tab and as the main headline in search engine results. It's one of the most important on-page SEO elements. The **meta description** (`<meta name="description">`) provides a concise summary of your webpage's content.
