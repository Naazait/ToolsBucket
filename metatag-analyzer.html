    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

    What are meta tags and why are they important?

    Meta tags are snippets of text that describe a page's content. They don't appear on the page itself but only in the page's HTML code. Meta tags are crucial for SEO because they provide search engines with concise information about what your page is about. This helps search engines categorize and rank your content appropriately, and influences how your page appears in search results.

    What is a meta description and why is it important for SEO?

    A meta description is a brief summary of a page's content, typically 150-160 characters long. While not a direct ranking factor, a well-written meta description is vital for SEO as it can significantly improve your click-through rate (CTR) from search results. It acts as a mini-advertisement for your page, convincing users to click on your link. Search engines often display the meta description below the title in search results.

    How long should my title tag be?

    For optimal display in search engine results, your title tag should ideally be between 50-60 characters. If it's too long, search engines may truncate it, cutting off important keywords or branding. Our analyzer checks for this and provides recommendations.

    Can meta keywords help my SEO?

    While meta keywords were once a significant SEO factor, their importance has diminished significantly. Major search engines like Google largely ignore the meta keywords tag due to past abuse. However, some smaller search engines or specific industry directories might still use them. We include it in our analysis for a complete picture, but recommend focusing your efforts on other, more impactful SEO elements.

    What are Open Graph and Twitter Card tags?

    Open Graph (OG) and Twitter Card tags are special meta tags that control how your web page content is displayed when shared on social media platforms like Facebook, LinkedIn, and Twitter. They allow you to specify the title, description, and image that appear in the shared preview. Properly implemented OG and Twitter Card tags make your shared links more appealing and professional, increasing engagement and click-throughs from social media.

    Why is an H1 heading important, and how many should I have?

    The H1 heading is considered the most important heading on a page. It typically contains the main topic or title of your content. For SEO and user experience, it's a best practice to have only one H1 heading per page. This helps search engines understand the primary subject of your page and improves readability for users by clearly outlining the main topic.

    What is a canonical URL and why do I need one?

    A canonical URL is a meta tag that tells search engines which version of a specific URL is the "master" version. This is crucial for avoiding duplicate content issues, which can arise when the same content is accessible via multiple URLs (e.g., with/without "www", different parameters, etc.). By specifying a canonical URL, you consolidate ranking signals to a single preferred version of your page, preventing search engines from being confused and potentially splitting your SEO authority.
