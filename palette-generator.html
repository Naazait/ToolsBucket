Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: What is a HEX code?
A: A HEX code is a hexadecimal format used to specify colors, primarily in web design and digital graphics. It consists of a '#' followed by six alphanumeric characters (e.g., #FFFFFF for white, #000000 for black). Our generator provides these codes for easy integration into your projects.
Q: Can I generate more than five colors?
A: Currently, our generator focuses on five-color palettes to provide a balanced and manageable set of options. For many design purposes, a five-color scheme offers ample versatility without becoming overwhelming.
Q: Is this tool truly free?
A: Absolutely! Our Color Palette Generator is 100% free to use, with no hidden costs or subscriptions. Enjoy unlimited color generation at no charge.
Q: How do I ensure my chosen colors are accessible (e.g., for visually impaired users)?
A: While our tool helps you generate palettes, it's good practice to run your final color combinations through an accessibility checker (like a contrast checker) to ensure sufficient contrast for text and important elements, especially for web projects. This helps make your designs usable by everyone.
Q: What's the best way to choose colors for a brand?
A: When choosing colors for a brand, consider the brand's personality, target audience, and industry. Research color psychology to understand the emotions different colors evoke. Start with a primary color, then use our generator to find complementary or accent colors that build a cohesive brand identity. Don't forget to consider how the colors will appear across various media.
