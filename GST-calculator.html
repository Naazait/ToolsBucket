Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) about GST

What is GST?

The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. It has replaced many older indirect taxes such as VAT, service tax, and excise duty, creating a unified market under the principle of 'One Nation, One Tax'. GST was introduced to simplify the indirect tax structure, reduce the cascading effect of taxes, and boost economic growth.

What are the different GST rates in India?

In India, the GST Council has categorized goods and services into several tax slabs to ensure equitable taxation. Primarily, there are five major GST rate slabs: 0% (for essential commodities like certain food grains, fresh vegetables, etc.), 5% (for items like packaged food, certain medicines, etc.), 12% (for processed foods, mobile phones, etc.), 18% (for most services, capital goods, many industrial intermediates), and 28% (for luxury items, sin goods like tobacco, aerated drinks, and certain automobiles). Our calculator supports all these official rates, allowing you to get precise calculations for any category of product or service.

What is the difference between CGST, SGST, and IGST?

Understanding the different components of GST is key for compliance:

  • CGST (Central GST): This is the tax collected by the Central Government on an intra-state sale (i.e., a transaction occurring within the boundaries of a single state or Union Territory). For example, if a product is sold from Mumbai to Pune (both in Maharashtra), both CGST and SGST will apply.
  • SGST (State GST): This is the tax collected by the State Government on an intra-state sale. It is levied concurrently with CGST, and the revenue is shared equally between the Central and State governments. In the Mumbai-Pune example, SGST would also be applied.
  • IGST (Integrated GST): This is the tax collected by the Central Government for an inter-state sale (i.e., a transaction between two different states or Union Territories) or on imports. IGST is designed to ensure a seamless flow of input tax credit across states. For instance, if a product is sold from Maharashtra to Gujarat, IGST would be applicable instead of CGST + SGST.

Our calculator focuses on intra-state transactions by showing the CGST and SGST split, where the total GST is divided equally between the two, providing transparent insight into tax distribution.

What is a reverse GST calculation (Remove GST)?

A reverse GST calculation, often referred to as 'Remove GST,' is a method used when you have a final price that already includes GST, and you need to determine the original price of the goods or services before the tax was applied, as well as the exact GST amount embedded within that final price. This is particularly useful in scenarios such as:

  • Retail Pricing: When a product has an MRP, and you need to know its ex-GST value for accounting or profit analysis.
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC): Businesses need to identify the GST component to claim ITC effectively.
  • Understanding Costs: For consumers, it helps in understanding how much tax they are paying on a particular product or service.

Our calculator simplifies this complex calculation significantly using the 'Remove GST' option, giving you clear insights into the tax component of GST-inclusive prices.
